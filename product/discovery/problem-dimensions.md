# Problem Structure

This document describes the structured formulation of the problem that the **Mini-CRM Rural** project aims to address.

The problem is analyzed from three complementary perspectives:

- Operational dimension
- Economic dimension
- Value chain dimension

This structure helps maintain clarity during product discovery, ensuring that the **initial product scope remains focused on operational problems**, while also acknowledging broader systemic issues in the rural economy.

---

# Primary Problem Statement

Small producers of fresh food who conduct direct and recurring sales at the local scale, seeking to increase their profit margins, frequently manage customers, orders, sales, and commercial communication in an informal manner (notes, memory, or scattered applications), making it difficult to organize operations, track sales, and develop direct channels with buyers.

Additionally, the absence of structured and reliable data regarding production, sales, income, and operational risks in this segment makes it difficult for financial institutions and insurers to properly price rural credit and insurance, limiting these producers' access to essential financial instruments.

---

# Operational Version of the Problem

Small producers of fresh food who conduct direct and recurring sales at the local scale frequently manage customers, orders, sales, and commercial communication informally—through notes, memory, or scattered applications—which makes it difficult to organize their operations, track sales, and maintain consistent relationships with buyers.

## Nature of the Problem

Operational management of daily commercial activities.

### Typical Symptoms

- Orders recorded in notebooks or messaging apps
- Lack of customer history
- Difficulty tracking sales
- Fragmented communication with buyers
- Limited visibility over recurring customers

## Relation to Mini-CRM Rural

This is the **core problem directly addressed by the Mini-CRM Rural MVP**.

The system aims to provide simple tools to organize:

- customer records
- orders
- sales history
- commercial communication

The goal is to structure operational information without introducing unnecessary complexity.

## Relation to MercadoRaiz (Future)

By organizing operational data, the system may gradually generate structured datasets regarding:

- sales activity
- client relationships
- product demand
- transaction patterns

These datasets could later support broader ecosystem services.

---

# Economic Version of the Problem

Small producers who attempt to increase their profit margins through direct sales frequently operate without structured tools for commercial management, reducing their ability to track revenue, organize orders, and develop stable commercial relationships with local buyers.

## Nature of the Problem

Economic inefficiency and limited financial visibility.

### Typical Consequences

- Untracked or poorly recorded revenues
- Difficulty identifying recurring clients
- Limited financial planning
- Reduced predictability of income
- Informal or undocumented business activity

These factors can also contribute to limited access to financial services.

## Relation to Mini-CRM Rural

The Mini-CRM can help producers:

- track sales
- organize customer relationships
- record recurring transactions
- obtain a clearer picture of their economic activity

This increases **economic visibility** of the producer’s operations.

## Relation to MercadoRaiz (Future)

Aggregated economic data may later support:

- regional demand analysis
- pricing intelligence
- financial modeling for rural activity
- alternative credit risk assessment

---

# Value Chain Version of the Problem

Small producers of fresh food often depend on intermediaries to access consumer markets because they face difficulties organizing direct sales, managing customers, and structuring their commercial operations efficiently.

## Nature of the Problem

Structural inefficiencies within the food supply chain.

### Typical Consequences

- dependence on intermediaries
- reduced profit margins for producers
- limited visibility of local production
- inefficient distribution of fresh food

## Relation to Mini-CRM Rural

The Mini-CRM does **not directly solve value chain coordination**, but it can strengthen producers' ability to:

- organize direct sales
- maintain commercial relationships
- manage buyer networks

This increases the producer’s operational autonomy.

## Relation to MercadoRaiz (Future)

If expanded beyond operational tools, the platform could potentially support:

- discovery of local producers
- connections between producers and buyers
- improved coordination of local food distribution
- broader market intelligence regarding supply and demand

---

# Strategic Interpretation

The three perspectives describe different layers of the same problem.

| Dimension | Type of Problem | Role of Mini-CRM Rural | Possible Future Role |
|----------|----------------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Operational | Daily management of commercial activity | Directly addressed by the system | Generates structured data |
| Economic | Limited visibility of revenue and activity | Improves financial awareness | Enables economic analysis |
| Value Chain | Structural inefficiencies in distribution | Strengthens producer autonomy | May support ecosystem coordination |

Maintaining this distinction helps ensure that the project **remains focused on the operational problem during early development**, while still recognizing the broader systemic context in which the solution operates.
